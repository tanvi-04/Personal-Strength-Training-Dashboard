# 🏋️‍♀️ Strength Training Performance Analytics

## Project Background

Tracking workouts manually often produces data that goes unused.  
This project analyzes **5 months of personal workout data (Oct 2025 – Mar 2026)** to identify patterns in training consistency, strength progression, and workout intensity.

The goal of the project is to demonstrate how **data analytics and visualization can transform personal activity logs into actionable insights**.

An interactive **Tableau dashboard** was developed to analyze trends and highlight performance metrics.

🔗 **Interactive Dashboard:** [https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/tanvi.patel1400/viz/PersonalStrengthTrainingAnalytics/Dashboard1]  

---

# Data Structure & Overview

The dataset consists of daily workout logs containing exercise-level data.

| Column | Description |
|------|------|
| Date | Workout date |
| Workout Type | Training split (Upper / Lower) |
| Exercise | Exercise performed |
| Muscle Group | Target muscle group |
| Sets | Number of sets |
| Reps | Repetitions per set |
| Weight_lbs | Weight lifted |
| Volume | Calculated training load (Weight × Reps) |

Key characteristics of the dataset:

- Missing workout days
- Fluctuating weight progression
- Variable number of sets and reps
- Occasional skipped exercises

This structure reflects **real-world behavioral data rather than perfectly clean datasets**.

---

# Executive Summary

The analysis shows that **consistent training patterns correlate with gradual strength progression and increased training volume over time**.

Across the five-month period:

- Workout consistency remained relatively stable
- Training volume increased gradually
- Strength progression improved across multiple exercises
- Certain muscle groups received significantly higher training volume

The dashboard enables quick monitoring of these trends.

---

# Key Insights

### 1. Strength Progression
Average lifting weight increased over the tracked period, indicating gradual improvement in strength performance.

### 2. Training Volume Growth
Weekly training volume increased steadily, suggesting progressive overload in the training program.

### 3. Muscle Group Distribution
Lower body muscle groups received the highest training volume compared to arms and shoulders.

### 4. Consistency Trends
Workout consistency remained a strong driver of performance improvement across the training period.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the observed trends:

- Continue maintaining a **consistent training schedule** to support long-term strength development.
- Increase **training intensity for underrepresented muscle groups**, particularly arms.
- Maintain progressive overload strategies to ensure continued strength gains.
- Track additional performance metrics such as recovery time and fatigue levels for deeper analysis.

---

# Assumptions & Caveats

- The dataset represents **self-logged workouts**, which may contain occasional inconsistencies.
- Strength improvement is inferred from **weight progression**, not formal strength testing.
- Missing days may represent rest days or unlogged workouts.
- The analysis assumes that **volume and weight progression are indicators of performance improvement**.
